Conclusion

Color     and     shape     are     two     observables     measured     by     black/gold     and     red/blue     boxes     respectively.     An     electron's     shape     can     either     be     diamond     or     square     and     an     electrons     color     can     either     be     red     or     blue.

States     of     electrons    
can     be     represented     as    
vectors     and     measurement    
devices     can     be     represented   
as     matrices.     Electrons     in     a     state     of     definite     color    are     eigenvectors     of     the     color     operator     and    
similarly     electrons     in     a     state     of     definite     shape     are     eigenvectors     of    the     shape     operator

The     incompatibility     of     color     and     shape     operators     is     shown     below     to     the     left     by     the     fact     that     the     state     of     color     is     not     an     eigenvector     of     the     shape     operator.     Likewise     shape     is     not     an     eigenvector     of     the     color     operator.     To     the     right     we     show     how     sums     and     differences     of     vectors     denote     superpositions     of     physical    states

In     the     above     experiment     we     are     initially     sending     an     electron     in     a     state     of     definite     color     (red)     into     a     shape     box.     Note     that     both     mirrors     in     either     corner     of     the     grid     only     redirect     the     path     of     the     electron     and     do     not     change     its     properties.     Below     we     run     the     same     experiment     except     we     replace     one     of    the    paths     with     a     wall     such     that    no     electrons    incident    on    that    path   can    get    through.     This     changes     the     result     of     the     experiment     from     100%     red     to     50%     red     and     50%     blue

Discussion

Given     the     surprising     result     of     experiment     two     we    want    to     use    experiment     three     to     help    
us     determine     how     the     electron    traveled     through     the     apparatus     in     experiment     two.     We     know    
that     the     only     difference     between     both     experiments     is     the     imposition     of     a     wall     along     the     cube    
path     in     experiment     three.    It     is     shocking     that     electrons     incident     on     the     diamond     path     in     experiment     three     have     different     color     properties     than     experiment     two    when     a     wall    is     placed     on     the     cube     path.     Logically     the     only     way     the     result    of     experiment     two     would     differ     from     experiment     three     would     be     if     the     electron     wasn't     just     traveling     down     the     diamond     path     and     was     therefore     affected     by     the     placement     of     a     wall     along     the     cube     path.     Let's     look     at     the     possibilities    that     exist     for     the     path     taken     by     the     electron    through     the     apparatus     in     experiment     two.    

a.)     The    electron     took    the    diamond    path =     This     can’t    be    true    as    described    above    given     the    result     of    experiment    two    is    different    from     experiment    three   

b.)    the    electron    took    the    cube    path =    Using     the    same    logic    above    and    now    placing    the    wall    on     the    diamond    path    instead    of    the    cube    path,    we    know    this    also    can’t    be    true   

c.)     the     electron     took     both     paths =     This     option     has     been     tested     by    placing    detectors    on    both    paths     and    the    result    is    that    an    electron    is    always    seen    on     either    the    cube    path    or    the    diamond    path    never    both    simultaneously    

d.)    The    electron    took     neither    paths =    We    place    a    wall    on    both    paths     and    surprisingly    no    electrons    come    out    either    end
